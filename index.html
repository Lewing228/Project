Sandomierz

Sandomierz is one of the oldest and historically most significant cities in Poland. Archeological finds around the city indicate that humans have inhabited the area since neolithic times. The city came into existence in the early Middle Ages, taking advantage of an excellent location at the junction of Vistula and San rivers, and on the path of important trade routes.

The first known historical mention of the city comes from the early 12th-century, when the chronicler Gallus Anonymus ranked it together with Kraków and Wrocław as one of the main cities of Poland. The testament (ca 1115-1118) of Bolesław III Wrymouth, in which he divided Poland among his sons, designated Sandomierz as the capital of one of the resulting principalities, the Duchy of Sandomierz.

Kozy

The town is located on the streams : Czerwonka , Kozówka , Pisarzówka and Leśniówka , in the Silesian Foothills and the northern slopes of the Little Beskids , on the road from Bielsko-Biała to Kraków. It borders Bielsko-Biała in the west, Pisarzowice in the north, Kęty in the north-east, Bujaków in the east , and Międzybrodzie Bialskie and Wilkowice in the south

The highest point is Groniczki - they are 833 m above sea level. Above the Goats there is a 35-meter millennium cross, standing on the top of Chrobacza Łąka (828 m above sea level), consecrated by Fr. Bishop Tadeusz Rakoczy in 2001. Kozy has a transport connection with Bielsko-Biała ( PKS buses , train). There is a Kozy train station in the village and a Kozy Zagroda stop on the railway line No. 117 . The town is not served by MZK buses from Bielsko , however, the line no. 6 (Lipnik Granica stop) and the line no. 31 run to the border between Bielsko and Kozy. The national road no. 52 runs through the village.

Warsaw

It was granted city rights before 1300 [6] . In 1569, by virtue of the Union of Lublin , Warsaw was established as the place of sessions of the Seyms of the Polish- Lithuanian Commonwealth [a] . From 1573, free elections took place there . After 1596, the royal court and central offices were moved to Warsaw, and in 1611, King Sigismund III Vasa settled permanently in the extended Royal Castle . The place of meetings of the general assemblies of the Mazowieckie Voivodeship and the land assemblies of the Warsaw region from the 16th century to the first half of the 18th century

Warsaw is the largest city in Poland in terms of population and area . It is also the only Polish city whose political system is defined by a separate act . Since 2002, it has been an urban commune with the status of a city with poviat status . It consists of 18 auxiliary units - districts of the Capital City of Warsaw
